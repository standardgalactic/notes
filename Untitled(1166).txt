== Scientific Work ==
In 1885, he became the assistant of [[de:Friedrich Nobbe]], an expert in seed quality control at the [[Tharandt]] Plant Physiology Research Station. Together, they conducted studies on the symbiotic relationships of the legume family [[Fabaceae]] with certain soil bacteria living on the [[Root nodule|root nodules]], following the work of [[Hermann Hellriegel]] and [[de:Hermann Wilfarth]]. These studies led to the development of the first cultures of [[Rhizobium|rhizobium bacteria]]<ref>{{Cite book |last=Friedrich Nobbe, Lorenz Hiltner |url= |title=Versuche über die Stickstoff–assimilation von Leguminosen. |date=1891|language= german}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |last=F. Nobbe |date=1893 |title=Impfet den Boden! |url= |journal=Sächsische landwirtschaftliche Zeitschrift |language=german |page= |issn=}}</ref>.  While in Tharandt, he also conducted studies  on the importance of root nodules in the nitrogen nutrition of [[black alder]].<ref>{{Cite book |last=Lorenz Hiltner |url= |title=Über die Bedeutung der Wurzelknöllchen von Alnus glutinosa für die Stickstoffernährung dieser Pflanze. |date=1894 |publisher= |isbn= |page= |pages= |language=Allemand}}</ref>.

At the same time, Hiltner prepared a thesis on "the diseases of plants of horticultural and agronomic interest caused by the fungus Botrytis cinerea and their treatment," which he presented in 1892 at the [[University of Erlangen–Nuremberg|University of Erlangen]], earning him a doctorate.

In 1902, he became the director of the Royal Institute of Agriculture and Botany in Munich (Königliche Agrikulturbotanische Anstalt, which would become in 1917 the Bavarian Institute for Plant Growth and Protection).<ref>{{Cite web |date= |title=Institut für Pflanzenschutz |url=https://www.lfl.bayern.de/ips/index.php 
 |website= |language=german}}</ref>

He was appointed professor at the [[Technical University of Munich]] in 1903, where he later earned the title of [[Emeritus|Professor Emeritus]] in Agricultural Bacteriology .

He first introduced the term "rhizosphere" during a lecture at the [[German Agricultural Society]] in April 1904, defining it as the region of soil directly influenced by root secretions and associated soil microorganisms.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Lorenz Hiltner |url= |title=Über neuere Erfahrungen und Probleme auf
